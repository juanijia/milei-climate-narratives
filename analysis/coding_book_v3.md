## Scope and Data Sources

The analytical framework distinguishes between **two separate data sources**, each analysed with its own dimension:

| Data Source | Analytical Dimension | Coding Book |
|---|---|---|
| Presidential speeches, interviews, public discourses | **Dimension 1 — Discursive Delegitimization** | This document (v3) |
| Formal documentation (executive decrees, budget documents, reorganization records, official policy instruments) | **Dimension 2 — State Capability Erosion** | See separate D2 protocol |

**This codebook (v3) covers speech coding only.** Dimension 2 is documented separately and applied exclusively to formal documentation. Do not apply D2 codes to speeches, even when a speech references, justifies, or announces an institutional change — those passages are coded under Dimension 1 (they are rhetorical acts, not the institutional acts themselves).

> **Why the separation matters:** A speech announcing withdrawal from the 2030 Agenda is a discursive act — it delegitimizes multilateral governance and rhetorically frames an exit. The actual institutional act is the executive decree or official communication. Conflating the two would confuse the mechanism: D1 captures *how Milei legitimizes dismantling through rhetoric*; D2 captures *what is actually dismantled through formal action*. The article's dual mechanism is demonstrated by triangulating both data sources independently, then showing their co-articulation.

## The Abductive Approach

This is an **abductive (inductive-deductive)** framework: the seed categories below are deductive starting points derived from the theoretical literature. Emergent patterns from the data are captured in Phase 2 (Inductive). The v1/v2 inductive categories (Civilizational_Defense, Selective_Empiricism, Temporal_Appropriation, Reported_Denial) are carried forward.

## Phase 1: Deductive (Seed Categories)

### Dimension 1 — Discursive Delegitimization of Sustainability Governance and Public Expertise

This dimension captures how presidential discourse **problematizes, delegitimizes, or reframes** the role of the state, sustainability governance, and scientific expertise. The key mechanism is **contested legitimacy**: Milei's rhetoric weakens the public and epistemic grounds for state action on climate, making that action politically and intellectually untenable — regardless of whether formal commitments remain in place.

Three sub-dimensions capture distinct rhetorical registers:

---

#### 1.1 — Delegitimization of State Intervention and Sustainability Governance

Passages where Milei delegitimizes the state as a legitimate actor in sustainability or environmental governance — not necessarily by denying climate change per se, but by denying the state's authority, effectiveness, or moral standing to act on it.

**Sub-codes:**

- **Market_Primacy**  
  Arguments asserting that free markets solve or manage environmental problems better than state coordination. Includes framing environmental outcomes as spontaneous results of market processes, property rights, or voluntary agreements. The key move: any problem addressed by state climate policy is better addressed by markets; therefore, the state has no legitimate role.  
  *Recognize by:* claims that capitalism eliminates poverty/environmental problems; invocation of Kirznerian market discovery; categorical denial that market failures exist.

- **Anti_State_Anti_Bureaucratic**  
  Rhetoric attacking the state apparatus, bureaucracy, or public administration as inherently inefficient, predatory, parasitic, or coercive — applied to environmental or sustainability agencies. Distinct from Anti_Casta (which is a political-class critique) because this targets the *organizational form* of the state, not a specific political elite.  
  *Recognize by:* metaphors of bureaucratic bloat ("hypertrofia"), images of comfortable bureaucrats living off others' labor, intervention spirals.

- **Anti_Casta**  
  Deployment of the "la casta" frame to delegitimize environmental institutions, climate ministries, sustainability technocrats, or global governance elites as part of the extractive political establishment. The mechanism: these actors do not serve the public but exploit governance positions for personal enrichment or ideological projects.  
  *Recognize by:* explicit "la casta" language; "casta privilegiada" applied to global governance actors; framing of institutional actors as parasites rather than public servants.

- **Delegitimization_of_Regulation**  
  Arguments casting environmental regulation or public coordination mechanisms (carbon pricing, emissions standards, permits, monitoring requirements) as illegitimate interference in private activity or market dynamics. Goes beyond arguing regulations are *costly* — claims they are categorically illegitimate (coercive, theoretically unfounded, or structurally socialist).  
  *Recognize by:* the "market failure is an oxymoron" argument; framing regulation as violence or coercion; claims that neoclassical externality theory is theoretically corrupt.

- **Deregulation_Benefits**  
  Narratives emphasizing the positive economic or social effects of removing environmental or sustainability-related regulations. The positive case for deregulation: removing regulations restores price-signal clarity, economic calculation, investment, and growth.  
  *Recognize by:* explicit claims that deregulation generates growth; the "don't pester innovators" argument; Europe as cautionary tale of regulatory overreach killing growth.

- **Sustainability_as_Ideological_or_Harmful**  
  Framing sustainability governance, climate policy, or the green agenda as ideologically motivated, economically damaging, or socially harmful. Distinct from outright denial (which disputes the environmental problem); this code applies when Milei acknowledges a legitimate environmental concern but frames the policy response as ideological or harmful regardless.  
  *Recognize by:* "policies that attribute climate change to humans are false"; characterizing the 2030 Agenda as producing "malos resultados"; framing climate policy as anti-development; Malthusian-anti-humanism charge.

- **Rejection_of_Multilateral_Governance**  
  Rejection of international sustainability agreements, the 2030 Agenda, multilateral climate bodies, or global governance institutions as illegitimate impositions on national sovereignty, expressions of globalist ideology, or instruments of ideological coercion. Applied when Milei *rhetorically frames* multilateral governance as illegitimate — regardless of whether a formal withdrawal has occurred.  
  *Recognize by:* declarations that the 2030 Agenda is "supranational government, socialist in nature"; calls to "drain" supranational organizations; invitations for a counter-coalition; characterizing multilateral bodies as "ideologues of barbarity."

---

#### 1.2 — Delegitimization of Public Science and Expertise

Passages where Milei delegitimizes public scientific institutions, academic knowledge, or expert authority — undermining the epistemic infrastructure that justifies sustainability policy.

**Sub-codes:**

- **Attack_on_Public_Research_Institutions**  
  Criticism of public research bodies (e.g., CONICET) as wasteful, ideologically captured, unproductive, or parasitic. These institutions provide the knowledge base for regulatory intervention and collective climate action; attacking them erodes the epistemic foundations of climate governance.  
  *Recognize by:* naming CONICET or equivalent bodies as wasteful; characterizing public researchers as rent-seekers funded by taxpayers.

- **Attack_on_Universities**  
  Criticism of public universities or academic sectors as centers of ideological indoctrination, inefficiency, or rent-seeking. Universities are the primary sites where climate scientists are trained and the expert community underpinning IPCC reports is formed.  
  *Recognize by:* framing universities as "indoctrination centers"; characterizing academic culture as producing ideologically captured rather than reliable knowledge.

- **Attack_on_Experts_and_Technocrats**  
  Delegitimization of expert authority more broadly — scientists, economists, policy specialists — as an elite that imposes knowledge on society rather than serving it. Distinct from Attack_on_Public_Research_Institutions (which targets specific institutions) because this targets the *authority claim* of experts as a class.  
  *Recognize by:* "vagos socialistas que escriben papers de cuarta"; "amparados en un marco teórico equivocado"; framing of international organizations' experts as "international bureaucrats."

- **Climate_Skepticism_or_Dismissal**  
  Explicit or implicit dismissal of climate science, environmental science, or the scientific basis for sustainability governance. Includes direct denial, natural cycles arguments, relativization of findings, or claims that attribution science is false.  
  *Recognize by:* five-cycles argument; "all policies that blame humans are false"; claiming science supports the skeptic position ("science and data are on our side").

- **Pseudo_Science_or_Ideological_Science**  
  Accusations that climate science, environmental research, or sustainability studies constitute pseudoscience, ideologically motivated inquiry, or politically captured knowledge. The move: not that specific findings are wrong, but that the entire institutional production of climate knowledge is epistemically corrupt.  
  *Recognize by:* "amparados en un marco teórico equivocado" applied structurally to the scientific community; neo-Marxist cooptation of universities and international organizations as producing ideological rather than reliable knowledge; the "Flat Earthers" inversion.

- **Rejection_of_Expert_Mediation**  
  Arguments that expert bodies should not mediate or influence policymaking — that decisions should be left to markets, individuals, or democratic majorities rather than technocratic or scientific elites. Distinct from attacking specific experts: this targets the *institutional role* of expertise in governance.  
  *Recognize by:* framing all epistemic institutions (universities, governance organizations, NGOs, media) as nodes in an ideological network that should be bypassed; arguing the state should not act based on expert consensus.

#### 1.3 — Woke / Cultural Backlash

Passages where sustainability, environmentalism, or climate governance is embedded within a broader culture-war framing — linked to gender ideology, neo-Marxism, or civilizational threat. The mechanism is **ideological bundling**: associating climate policy with a broader political enemy, removing it from its own empirical domain and relocating it within a unified ideological target.

**Sub-codes:**

- **Woke_Bundling**  
  Merging climate, gender, DEI, or sustainability into a single "woke" enemy. The rhetorical move treats these as co-expressions of the same collectivist-progressive project, allowing the emotional and moral charge of one issue to contaminate another.  
  *Recognize by:* enumerating environmentalism alongside feminism, gender ideology, or immigration as "heads of the same beast"; "Wokism also manifests in... radical environmentalism"; Malthus-abortion-climate chain.

- **Neo_Marxism**  
  Framing environmentalism or the green agenda explicitly as disguised socialism, Marxism, or collectivism — "watermelon politics" (green outside, red inside). The claim: climate governance is not a genuine environmental response but a strategic vehicle for socialist state expansion.  
  *Recognize by:* "the conflict socialists propose is man against nature"; environmental movement as "new agenda" of neo-Marxists after failing with class struggle; Malthusian premise as disguised population-control socialism.

- **Civilizational_Defense**  
  A master framing that positions Western civilization as under existential threat from socialism/collectivism, with climate governance as one front in that civilizational war. Operates at a higher level of abstraction than Neo_Marxism (which labels ideological content) — here the stakes are civilizational survival itself.  
  *Recognize by:* "Occidente está en peligro"; the three-year Davos arc as civilizational recovery narrative; America-as-beacon framing; Trump-Milei alignment as civilizational vanguard.

## Phase 2: Inductive (Emergent Categories)

The following categories were generated inductively during prior coding passes and are carried forward. Apply them when the pattern recurs; note both the INDUCTIVE label and the closest D1 sub-code.

---

### Selective_Empiricism
**Definition:** A rhetorical pattern in which Milei deploys the language of empirical authority exclusively for pro-market claims, while simultaneously characterizing opposing institutional knowledge (including climate science) as ideologically captured and invalid. Distinct from Pseudo_Science_or_Ideological_Science because here the move is to *appropriate* empiricism for one side while denying it to the other — not merely labelling science as ideological, but crowding it out with a rival empirical claim.

**Closest D1 code:** 1.2 > Pseudo_Science_or_Ideological_Science — apply as INDUCTIVE when the dual-move (appropriating + denying empiricism simultaneously) is the dominant feature.

**Example:** *"La evidencia empírica es incuestionable"* (about capitalism's poverty record), immediately followed by dismissal of "líderes, pensadores y académicos... amparados en un marco teórico equivocado" — Davos 2024.

---

### Temporal_Appropriation
**Definition:** A rhetorical strategy in which Milei co-opts the normative language of intergenerational responsibility — the moral foundation of sustainability discourse — and redeploys it to argue *against* state intervention and multilateral climate governance. The sustainability regime's own moral vocabulary (futurity, sacrifice-of-present, protecting future generations) is turned against it.

**Closest D1 code:** 1.1 > Rejection_of_Multilateral_Governance — apply as INDUCTIVE when the temporal-moral register appropriation is the dominant feature, not just a rejection of multilateral legitimacy.

**Example:** *"Incendian el futuro para mantener caliente el presente"* used to frame the 2030 Agenda as itself the actor sacrificing future welfare — UN 80th Assembly, 2025.

---

### Reported_Denial
**Definition:** An opposing speaker's reported-speech paraphrase of a Milei climate statement, coded as inter-discursive corroboration of a primary position. Apply when a political opponent accurately summarizes a Milei climate-related claim in a public forum, confirming the position circulates and is publicly understood. **Not primary discourse** — function is confirmatory and contextual only.

**Closest D1 code:** 1.3 > Neo_Marxism (in the documented instance). Apply with INDUCTIVE prefix and note it is not primary Milei discourse.

**Example:** *"Llegó a decir que el cambio climático es del socialismo"* — Bregman's paraphrase during the 2023 Presidential Debate.

## Instructions for the Analyst Agent (v3)

1. **Read this codebook in full** before touching any speech files.
2. **Apply only Dimension 1 codes** (D1.1, D1.2, D1.3, and inductive categories) to speech files. Do not apply any D2 code to a speech, even if the speech announces or justifies an institutional action.
3. **When a speech references an institutional change** (e.g., "Argentina decided to exit the 2030 Agenda process"), code the passage under the appropriate D1 sub-code capturing *the rhetorical act* — typically D1.1 > Rejection_of_Multilateral_Governance, D1.1 > Sustainability_as_Ideological_or_Harmful, or D1.1 > Anti_State_Anti_Bureaucratic — not a D2 code.
4. **For inductive categories,** note both the INDUCTIVE label and the closest D1 sub-code in the annotation.
5. **Save coded outputs to** `analysis/coded_speeches/v3/`.
6. **Track frequency in** `analysis/coded_speeches/v3/frequency_tally_v3.json`.
7. **If a new pattern emerges** not covered by existing codes, follow the inductive category protocol: apply `[CODE: INDUCTIVE > TentativeName]`, document it, and add it here under Phase 2.
8. **After coding the full corpus**, generate `analysis/results/v3/final_analysis_report_v3.md`.
