# Final Analysis Report v3: Milei's Climate Narratives
**Version:** 3.0 | **Date:** 2026-06-08 | **Coded by:** Claude (discourse-coder-v3)

---

## Executive Summary

Across nine speeches and 98 coded annotations spanning October 2023 to March 2026, Milei's climate obstruction rhetoric is overwhelmingly organized around the delegitimization of state authority over sustainability governance (D1.1: 58.2% of annotations), not around climate science denial — a pattern consistent with libertarian rather than populist-nativist climate obstruction in the comparative literature. The single most frequent code is `Rejection_of_Multilateral_Governance` (n=15), concentrated in the UN and Davos speeches where Milei addresses the very institutions he targets. Three distinct venue-specific rhetorical registers emerge: a philosophical-theoretical register for Davos audiences, a sovereignty-geopolitical register for the UN, and a deregulation-freedom register for domestic congressional addresses (*Aperturas*) — the last of which is markedly more direct in dismissing environmental concerns ("prejuicios ambientalistas absurdos") than the more theoretically mediated international speeches. The 2023–2026 arc shows a clear shift away from explicit climate science denial and toward increasingly sophisticated governance-foundational arguments, tracking Milei's transition from candidate to governing president.

## Dimension 1 — Discursive Delegitimization: Key Findings

D1.1 constitutes 58% of all annotations — a clear dominance that confirms the primary rhetorical mechanism is the delegitimization of *state authority over sustainability governance*, not skepticism of climate science per se. D1.3 (Cultural Backlash, 21%) functions as a meta-narrative that pre-loads the specific D1.1 and D1.2 arguments with civilizational stakes, operating especially in the international forum speeches. D1.2 (Science/Expertise, 14%) is the thinnest dimension, and this asymmetry is itself analytically significant.

### D1.1 — Delegitimization of State Intervention and Sustainability Governance

With 57 annotations across all nine speeches, D1.1 is the dominant rhetorical register of the corpus. The leading sub-codes tell a coherent theoretical story:

**`Rejection_of_Multilateral_Governance` (n=15, 15.3% of all annotations)** is the single most frequent code. Its concentration in the UN and Davos speeches (where Milei is literally addressing the targeted institutions) reflects a structural feature: the most direct attacks on multilateral governance happen at the most prominent multilateral forums. The arguments deployed here range from formal denunciation of the 2030 Agenda as "a supranational government program, socialist in nature" (UN 79th 2024), to an invitation to form a counter-coalition ("the agenda of freedom"), to a four-principle institutional redesign that would strip the UN of its sustainability mandate (UN 80th 2025). By 2026, Milei declares "the era of global cooperation without moral compass has ended" (*Apertura 2026*) — a claim that closes the door on multilateral climate governance not by opposing it but by announcing it is historically over.

**`Deregulation_Benefits` (n=10)** and **`Delegitimization_of_Regulation` (n=9)** together account for 19 of 57 D1.1 annotations. These operate through two complementary moves: `Delegitimization_of_Regulation` attacks the theoretical foundation of environmental regulation (market failures are "an oxymoron," externality theory is a Trojan horse for state intrusion, the Hoppe non-aggression principle categorically prohibits intervention), while `Deregulation_Benefits` makes the positive case (deregulation liberates increasing returns, restores price signals, generates growth). The *Apertura 2026* speech introduces the most aggressive domestic version of this pairing: an explicit commitment to "exterminar regulaciones" to remove "barreras legales" from hydrocarbon, mining, and agro sectors, dismissing environmental concerns as "prejuicios ambientalistas absurdos."

**`Market_Primacy` (n=7)** establishes the foundational claim: free-enterprise capitalism is "the only tool" for ending poverty, and market failures are logically impossible. For climate governance, this is a pre-emptive foreclosure — if markets are the only legitimate mechanism for collective problems, then state climate action is redundant by definition, before any empirical case for it can be made.

**`Anti_State_Anti_Bureaucratic` (n=5) and `Anti_Casta` (n=4)** personalize the institutional target. The bureaucrat "sitting in a luxury office" and the *casta* that "lives off what others produce" are the human face of the regulatory apparatus. The *Apertura 2024* speech institutionalizes the casta frame in a presidential address for the first time, applying it domestically in a way that will later be extended to CONICET researchers, environmental ministry officials, and multilateral governance technocrats.

### D1.2 — Delegitimization of Public Science and Expertise

D1.2's relatively low frequency (14%, lower than D1.3's 21%) is the most theoretically significant finding in the corpus. It confirms that Milei's obstruction strategy primarily targets *state authority* rather than *scientific consensus* — a distinctively libertarian pattern, as opposed to the populist-nativist obstruction pattern that leads with explicit climate denial and attacks on the IPCC.

**`Attack_on_Experts_and_Technocrats` (n=5)** is the dominant D1.2 sub-code, and tellingly, most instances frame experts as a political class ("international bureaucrats who impose a lifestyle," "lazy socialists writing fourth-rate papers") rather than engaging with their claims. This is a delegitimization of the *social role* of expertise, not a refutation of scientific findings.

**`Climate_Skepticism_or_Dismissal` (n=2)** appears only twice in the corpus, and both instances use the same five-temperature-cycles argument deployed in Davos 2025 — a natural cycles claim that co-exists with the disclaimer "I don't deny climate change." This rhetorical structure is analytically important: Milei performs non-denial while reproducing the logical content of denial. The rarity of explicit climate science denial (only 2 annotations across 98 total) is consistent with the D1.1-dominant pattern.

**`Pseudo_Science_or_Ideological_Science` (n=3)** frames the problem at the institutional rather than finding-specific level: academic and expert institutions have been "captured" by neo-Marxist ideology, so their outputs are epistemically contaminated as a class. This is structurally more powerful than contesting specific findings — it invalidates the knowledge-production infrastructure wholesale.

### D1.3 — Woke / Cultural Backlash

D1.3 operates as the meta-narrative that gives the D1.1 and D1.2 arguments civilizational stakes. Without D1.3, the anti-state and anti-regulation arguments could be read as technocratic or economic. With it, they become part of a defense of Western civilization against socialist capture.

**`Civilizational_Defense` (n=9)** appears across all three Davos speeches, both UN speeches, and the 2026 *Apertura* — the only sub-code with such broad distribution. In the international speeches, it establishes the master frame: Western civilization is under existential threat, and every subsequent argument (including against climate governance) inherits civilizational stakes. The 2026 *Apertura* represents its most institutionalized form: "la moral occidental" is declared the foundational policy criterion for Argentine statehood for the next 50 years.

**`Neo_Marxism` (n=6)** and **`Woke_Bundling` (n=6)** work together to contaminate climate governance by association. The neo-Marxism code establishes the theoretical claim: the green agenda is a tactical repositioning by socialists who abandoned classical class struggle ("el conflicto del hombre contra la naturaleza"). The woke-bundling code then delivers the contamination move: climate governance is listed alongside gender ideology, reproductive rights, and population control as "various heads of the same beast aimed at justifying state expansion." By 2025, Milei achieves the most sophisticated version of this bundling at Davos: "Wokism also manifests in sinister radical environmentalism" — a concession-then-perversion structure that is harder to dismiss as reactionary denial.

## Inductive Categories

### Selective_Empiricism (n=2, Davos 2024 + Davos 2025)

Milei claims unquestionable empirical authority for pro-market historical claims ("the evidence is unquestionable," "science and data are on our side") while simultaneously dismissing academic and institutional knowledge — including climate science — as ideologically captured. This is not straightforwardly coded under D1.1 or D1.2 because it operates at the level of epistemological *methodology*: it appropriates empiricist prestige for one side while denying it to the other, crowding out rather than refuting. With only 2 corpus instances, it does not yet meet the threshold for formalization as a permanent deductive sub-code, but it is analytically important as the bridge between D1.1's governance delegitimization and D1.2's science/expertise attacks.

### Temporal_Appropriation (n=3, UN 80th 2025 ×2, Apertura 2026 ×1)

The most analytically distinctive inductive finding and the strongest candidate for formalization as a permanent deductive code. Milei appropriates sustainability discourse's core moral vocabulary — intergenerational responsibility, protecting the future, not sacrificing tomorrow for today — and redirects it *against* sustainability governance. The mechanism is elegant: in UN 80th 2025, the claim that politicians "incendian el futuro para mantener caliente el presente" is borrowed directly from sustainability rhetoric, then deployed to frame the 2030 Agenda as the actor wasting scarce resources that future generations will need. In Apertura 2026, a "nación inmadura que dilapida el futuro para repartir beneficios en el presente" inverts climate governance's intergenerational moral claim: redistribution (including climate investment) sacrifices the future, while market freedom is what "plans for both present and future."

Three corpus instances exceed the informal threshold for formalizing Temporal_Appropriation. It is distinct from all existing D1 codes because it does not attack the legitimacy of sustainability governance by invoking alternative values, but by *mimicking* sustainability's own normative logic and weaponizing it. This is a more sophisticated rhetorical operation than straightforward delegitimization.

### Reported_Denial (n=1, Debate 2023)

A single inter-discursive instance: Bregman's reported-speech paraphrase "llegó a decir que el cambio climático es del socialismo" serves as public corroboration of the Neo_Marxism code circulating in Milei's pre-presidential discourse. Too rare to formalize.

## Rhetorical Evolution Across the Corpus (2023–2026)

The corpus reveals a consistent directional shift: from explicit climate science denial and ad hominem expert attacks (candidate register, 2023) toward increasingly sophisticated governance-foundational and philosophical arguments (governing president register, 2024–2026). This trajectory has both analytical and strategic dimensions.

**2023: The Candidate Register.** The Debate Presidencial 2023 is unique in the corpus for combining outright climate denial ("todas esas políticas que culpan al ser humano del cambio climático son falsas") with the rawest ad hominem expert attack ("vagos socialistas que escriben papers de cuarta"). Both instances are D1.2 — the only speech where D1.2 codes dominate. This candidate register is compressed, punchy, and designed for the domestic debate audience rather than international institutions.

**Early 2024: Theoretical Elaboration.** The Davos 2024 speech is the largest in the corpus (20 annotations) and the most theoretically developed. It introduces the full market-failure-impossibility argument, the Kirznerian market-as-discovery argument, and the neoclassical economics Trojan-horse claim — all from D1.1. The D1.3 meta-narrative is established: Western civilization is under existential threat from cooptación neo-marxista. This speech is essentially the theoretical manifesto from which subsequent speeches draw.

**Mid–Late 2024: Domestic Institutionalization.** The *Apertura 2024* institutionalizes key frames in a presidential address for the first time. The DNU derogation (including environmental regulations) is framed as "returning freedom." The casta frame is formalized. The UN 79th speech adds the South-South dimension for the developing-country UN audience: climate policy harms the poor, multilateral conditionality is extortion.

**2025: Civilizational Peak and Temporal Inversion.** Davos 2025 marks the highest D1.3 density in the corpus (7/20 codes, 35%). The five-cycles climate denial argument is deployed; the civilizational frame achieves maximum intensity ("sinister agenda," "barbarity," "golden calf idolatry"). The *Apertura 2025* is predominantly D1.1. The UN 80th 2025 introduces Temporal_Appropriation — the most sophisticated rhetorical move of the corpus.

**2026: The Victory Narrative.** By Davos 2026, the rhetoric shifts from warning to victory: "2026 is the year the world has begun to awaken." The philosophical arguments reach their most abstract form (Hoppe non-aggression principle, Huerta de Soto dynamic efficiency, the ethics-efficiency-utility values hierarchy). The *Apertura 2026* is the most analytically rich domestic speech, combining the most explicit direct dismissal of environmentalism in the corpus ("prejuicios ambientalistas absurdos"), a declaration that Western morality governs Argentine policy for 50 years, and a second instance of Temporal_Appropriation.

**The venue-register distinction.** A finding the original analytical anchors did not anticipate: the three *Apertura* speeches constitute a distinct third rhetorical register alongside the Davos and UN speeches. The Apertura register is: (1) primarily D1.1, almost no D1.2 or D1.3; (2) anchored in concrete domestic policy (DNU, sector-specific deregulation); (3) more direct in dismissing environmental concerns; (4) building in D1.3 over time (Apertura 2024: 0 D1.3; Apertura 2025: 1; Apertura 2026: 3). The Apertura 2026 passage "lejos de prejuicios ambientalistas absurdos" is the most unguarded dismissal of environmentalism in the entire corpus — a level of directness absent from the theoretically mediated international speeches. This suggests the culture-war framing, initially calibrated for international forums, is now entering the domestic register as Milei consolidates power.

## Co-occurrence Patterns and Rhetorical Bundles

The most analytically significant stacked-code patterns are:

**D1.3 > Neo_Marxism + D1.3 > Woke_Bundling** (Davos 2024, Davos 2025): The climate-is-socialism argument in a single move. These two codes consistently appear on the same passage because the bundling operation and the theoretical claim it rests on are simultaneous. The bundling (climate = woke = socialism) and the theoretical genealogy (neo-Marxist tactical repositioning) are structurally inseparable.

**D1.1 > Market_Primacy + D1.1 > Delegitimization_of_Regulation** (Davos 2024, Davos 2025): The positive and negative arms of the market-failure argument are frequently deployed together — "markets solve collective problems" and "regulation is theoretically illegitimate" are the same argument from opposite directions.

**D1.1 > Deregulation_Benefits + D1.1 > Delegitimization_of_Regulation** (Davos 2026 "violent and unjust"): The most philosophically compressed double in the corpus — one sentence that simultaneously argues regulation is morally wrong *and* economically harmful.

**D1.1 > Rejection_of_Multilateral_Governance + IND > Temporal_Appropriation** (UN 80th 2025): The most rhetorically sophisticated bundle. Milei appropriates sustainability's intergenerational moral claim and turns it against the 2030 Agenda in the same passage where he announces Argentina's exit from that agenda. The rhetorical opponent's moral vocabulary is weaponized against their institutional project.

**D1.1 > Sustainability_as_Ideological_or_Harmful + D1.1 > Delegitimization_of_Regulation** (Apertura 2026): In the domestic register, the most direct attack on environmental policy — "prejuicios ambientalistas absurdos" blocking resource extraction — is paired immediately with the regulatory-barriers argument. The practical policy consequence is stated alongside the ideological dismissal.

## Key Quotes and Exemplary Passages

**1. The Market-Failure Foreclosure (Davos 2024)**
> *"El mercado es un mecanismo de cooperación social donde se intercambian voluntariamente. Por lo tanto el fallo del mercado es un oxímoron. No existe fallo de mercado."*
> ["The market is a mechanism of social cooperation where things are exchanged voluntarily. Therefore market failure is an oxymoron. Market failures do not exist."]

Codes: D1.1 > Market_Primacy + D1.1 > Delegitimization_of_Regulation

This passage performs the foundational closure of the entire corpus. Environmental externalities — greenhouse gas emissions, pollution, resource depletion — are the paradigmatic market failures in environmental economics. By declaring them logically impossible (not empirically absent, but conceptually self-contradictory), Milei eliminates the theoretical ground for *any* climate regulation before any empirical evidence can be presented. It is not a scientific claim but a first-principles philosophical claim, and as such is immune to empirical refutation.

**2. The Casta-Climate Nexus (Apertura 2024)**
> *"La casta política es lo peor que le puede pasar a un país como el nuestro."*
> ["The political caste is the worst thing that can happen to a country like ours."]

Code: D1.1 > Anti_Casta

The first formalization of the casta indictment in a presidential address. Its relevance to climate governance is prospective: once the "casta" frame is institutionalized in a State of the Nation address, it provides the legitimating logic for subsequently categorizing environmental agencies, CONICET researchers, sustainability ministries, and multilateral governance bodies as members of the same parasitic class.

**3. The South-South Delegitimation (UN 79th 2024)**
> *"La tutela del Foro Económico Mundial no ha ayudado, donde se promueven políticas ridículas con anteojeras malthusianas — como las políticas de 'Cero Emisiones' — que perjudican en particular a los países pobres."*
> ["The tutelage of the World Economic Forum has not helped, where ridiculous policies are promoted with Malthusian blinders — such as 'Zero Emission' policies — which harm poor countries in particular."]

Codes: D1.1 > Sustainability_as_Ideological_or_Harmful + D1.3 > Woke_Bundling

The audience calibration here is precise. At the UN General Assembly, dominated by Global South nations, Milei repositions climate skepticism as a defense of developing countries against elite climate imperialism. "Malthusian blinders" bundles net-zero policy with the Malthusian population-control argument, which links it moments later to abortion — maximally toxic bundling for a developing-country majority audience.

**4. The Temporal Inversion (UN 80th 2025)**
> *"Incendian el futuro para mantener caliente el presente... la Argentina decidió en su momento apartarse de este proceso porque veíamos en esta agenda un mal gasto de recursos escasos."*
> ["They burn the future to keep the present warm... Argentina decided to step away from this process because we saw this agenda as a waste of scarce resources."]

Codes: D1.1 > Rejection_of_Multilateral_Governance + IND > Temporal_Appropriation

The most analytically sophisticated passage in the corpus. The opening metaphor ("burn the future") is borrowed directly from sustainability discourse's intergenerational moral frame, then applied to the 2030 Agenda itself: it is the sustainability apparatus that wastes scarce resources that future generations will need. The rhetorical move is structurally identical to judo — using the opponent's force (intergenerational moral claim) against their institutional project (2030 Agenda).

**5. The Apertura Dismissal (Apertura 2026)**
> *"Vamos a construir un marco legal robusto que permita el desarrollo primario para beneficio de todos los argentinos, con cuidados, pero lejos de prejuicios ambientalistas absurdos."*
> ["We will build a robust legal framework that allows primary development for the benefit of all Argentines, with care, but far from absurd environmentalist prejudices."]

Codes: D1.1 > Sustainability_as_Ideological_or_Harmful + D1.1 > Delegitimization_of_Regulation

The most direct anti-environmentalism statement in the corpus, significant precisely because it appears in the most formally domestic setting — a presidential address to Congress. "Prejuicios ambientalistas absurdos" frames environmental concerns not as competing legitimate interests but as pre-rational bias, and does so not through philosophical indirection but directly. The application to hydrocarbon, mining, fishing, and agriculture is explicit. This contrasts sharply with the international speeches, which tend to attack environmental governance through more theoretically mediated routes.

**6. Western Morality as Permanent Policy Architecture (Apertura 2026)**
> *"Dedicaremos este año a examinar la organización jurídica e institucional que nos trajo hasta aquí y construir una arquitectura nueva; la arquitectura que tendrá el Estado argentino los próximos 50 años, teniendo la moral occidental como política de estado."*
> ["We will dedicate this year to examining the legal and institutional organization that brought us to this point and building a new architecture; the architecture the Argentine state will have for the next 50 years, with Western morality as state policy."]

Code: D1.3 > Civilizational_Defense

The most expansive civilizational claim in the corpus. By anchoring the 50-year institutional architecture in "Western morality" rather than constitutional norms or democratic consensus, this passage constructs climate governance (which invokes collective obligations and international agreements) as fundamentally incompatible with the normative framework for all future Argentine policymaking. It attempts to make the libertarian-Western architecture normatively permanent — not a policy choice but a civilizational commitment.

## Notes for Triangulation with Dimension 2 (Corpus B)

The D1 coding documents the discursive layer; D2 (formal documentation) must document the institutional layer. The following D1 findings most urgently require D2 triangulation:

**1. 2030 Agenda withdrawal.** Multiple speeches (UN 79th 2024, UN 80th 2025) rhetorically frame Argentina's exit from the 2030 Agenda as principled and necessary. The D2 evidence — the formal UN communication, executive communications, voting records — would confirm or extend the rhetorical pattern with institutional evidence.

**2. DNU 70/23 environmental deregulation.** The *Apertura 2024* explicitly frames DNU 70/23 as "returning freedom through the derogation of senseless regulations." The D2 evidence is the DNU itself: which specific environmental and land-use regulations were revoked, and what their formal justification was. The D1 and D2 layers are already articulated here — the speech *announces* the institutional act.

**3. Supranational organization defunding.** The Davos 2025 call for "drastic reduction of all supranational organizations, draining their resources" requires triangulation with Argentina's actual financial contributions to UNEP, IPCC, and other multilateral sustainability bodies, and any budget cuts or withdrawal of contributions since December 2023.

**4. The "prejuicios ambientalistas absurdos" commitment (Apertura 2026).** The explicit promise to build a legal framework for resource extraction "far from absurd environmentalist prejudices" should be traceable in subsequent executive decrees, environmental impact assessment waivers, or legislative initiatives removing environmental protections.

## Conclusions and Next Steps

**Finding 1: Governance delegitimization is the primary mechanism.** D1.1 at 58% confirms that Milei's climate obstruction is organized primarily around the delegitimization of state authority over sustainability governance, not around climate science denial (D1.2 at 14%). This is consistent with a libertarian-foundational obstruction pattern and distinct from the alt-right/nativist pattern that leads with scientific denial.

**Finding 2: Rejection_of_Multilateral_Governance is the corpus's single most frequent code** (n=15, 15.3%), concentrated in the UN and Davos speeches. The counter-coalition strategy (inviting nations to join an "agenda of freedom") transforms Argentina from a passive defector from multilateral climate commitments into an active recruiter of an opposition bloc.

**Finding 3: Three distinct venue-specific registers exist.** The Davos register is philosophical-theoretical; the UN register is sovereignty-geopolitical (calibrated for the Global South); the Apertura register is domestic-deregulatory and increasingly direct about dismissing environmental concerns. This venue-register distinction is a new finding from the expanded corpus.

**Finding 4: The 2023→2026 arc runs from denial toward governance.** Explicit climate science denial is confined to 2023. By 2026, the arguments are primarily foundational-philosophical (property-rights proofs, values hierarchies). This shift tracks the transformation from candidate to governing president and suggests climate obstruction is becoming more institutionally embedded and less rhetorical.
