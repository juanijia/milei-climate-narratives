# Coding Book v2: Milei's Climate Narratives

**Version:** 2.0  
**Date:** June 2026  
**Based on:** Research design in `docs/Draft/draft_literaturereview_researchdesign.md`  
**Previous version:** `analysis/coding_book.md` (v1)

This document is the analytical framework for the **second coding pass** of the corpus. The v2 framework reorganizes the categories around two core theoretical dimensions drawn from the integration of climate obstruction, policy dismantling, and right-wing populism literatures: (1) the **narrative** dimension of discursive delegitimization, and (2) the **material-institutional** dimension of state capability erosion.

This is still an **abductive (inductive-deductive) framework**: the categories below are deductive seed codes derived from the theoretical framework. Emergent patterns from the data are captured in Phase 2 (Inductive). The v1 inductive categories (Civilizational_Defense_Frame, Selective_Empiricism, Temporal_Appropriation, Reported_Denial) are carried forward as candidate codes under the relevant pillars, since they were grounded in the corpus.

---

## Phase 1: Deductive (Seed Categories)

### Dimension 1 — Discursive Delegitimization of Sustainability Governance and Public Expertise

This dimension captures how presidential discourse problematizes, delegitimizes, or reframes the role of the state, sustainability governance, and scientific expertise. The key mechanism is **discursive delegitimation**: weakening the public and epistemic grounds for state action.

---

#### 1.1 Delegitimization of State Intervention and Sustainability Governance

Passages where Milei delegitimizes the state as an actor in sustainability or environmental governance — not by denying climate change per se, but by denying the state's authority, legitimacy, or effectiveness to act on it.

Sub-codes:

- **Market_Primacy**: Arguments asserting that free markets solve or manage environmental problems better than state coordination. Includes framing environmental outcomes as spontaneous results of market processes, property rights, or voluntary agreements.

- **Anti_State_Anti_Bureaucratic**: Rhetoric attacking the state apparatus, bureaucracy, or public administration as inefficient, predatory, parasitic, or coercive — applied to environmental or sustainability agencies.

- **Anti_Casta**: Deployment of the "la casta" frame to delegitimize environmental institutions, climate ministries, or sustainability technocrats as part of the extractive political establishment.

- **Delegitimization_of_Regulation**: Arguments casting environmental regulation or public coordination mechanisms (permits, standards, monitoring requirements) as illegitimate interference in private activity or market dynamics.

- **Deregulation_Benefits**: Narratives emphasizing the positive economic or social effects of removing environmental or sustainability-related regulations.

- **Sustainability_as_Ideological_or_Harmful**: Framing sustainability governance, climate policy, or the green agenda as ideologically motivated, economically damaging, or socially harmful — distinct from outright denial; the claim is that the policy is harmful even if the problem is real.

- **Rejection_of_Multilateral_Governance**: Rejection of international sustainability agreements, the 2030 Agenda, or multilateral climate bodies as illegitimate impositions on national sovereignty or expressions of globalist ideology.

---

#### 1.2 Delegitimization of Public Science and Expertise

Passages where Milei delegitimizes public scientific institutions, academic knowledge, or expert authority — undermining the epistemic infrastructure that justifies sustainability policy.

Sub-codes:

- **Attack_on_Public_Research_Institutions**: Criticism of public research bodies (e.g., CONICET) as wasteful, ideologically captured, or unproductive.

- **Attack_on_Universities**: Criticism of public universities or academic sectors as centers of ideological indoctrination, inefficiency, or rent-seeking.

- **Attack_on_Experts_and_Technocrats**: Delegitimization of expert authority more broadly — scientists, economists, policy specialists — as an elite that imposes knowledge on society rather than serving it.

- **Climate_Skepticism_or_Dismissal**: Explicit or implicit dismissal of climate science, environmental science, or the scientific basis for sustainability governance. Includes direct denial, relativization of findings, or assertions that models are unreliable.

- **Pseudo_Science_or_Ideological_Science**: Accusations that climate science, environmental research, or sustainability studies constitute pseudoscience, ideologically motivated inquiry, or politically captured knowledge — framing science itself as epistemically corrupt.

- **Rejection_of_Expert_Mediation**: Arguments that expert bodies should not mediate or influence policymaking — that decisions should be left to markets, individuals, or democratic majorities rather than technocratic or scientific elites.

---

#### 1.3 Woke/Cultural Backlash

Passages where sustainability, environmentalism, or climate governance is embedded within a broader culture-war framing — linked to gender ideology, neo-Marxism, or a civilizational threat. The mechanism here is **ideological bundling**: associating climate policy with a broader political enemy, rather than engaging with its substantive content.

Sub-codes:

- **Woke_Bundling**: Merging climate, gender, DEI, or sustainability into a single "woke" enemy. The rhetorical move treats these as expressions of the same collectivist-progressive project.

- **Neo_Marxism**: Framing environmentalism or the green agenda explicitly as disguised socialism, Marxism, or collectivism — "watermelon politics" (green outside, red inside). Note: distinct from Market_Primacy, which argues markets work better; this sub-code claims the environmental agenda is a strategic vehicle for socialism.

- **Civilizational_Defense**: A master framing that positions Western civilization as under existential threat from socialism/collectivism, with climate governance as one front in that civilizational war. Recognize by the presence of civilizational language ("Occidente está en peligro") framing specific policy debates as threats to Western existence. *Carried forward from v1 inductive category.*

---

### Dimension 2 — Erosion of State Capabilities for Sustainability Governance

This dimension captures institutional and material changes — expressed in discourse as justifications or announcements — affecting the state's capacity to design, coordinate, implement, finance, monitor, and enforce sustainability policy. Unlike Dimension 1 (which codes rhetoric), Dimension 2 codes discursive references to concrete institutional or budgetary actions.

**Important:** This dimension tracks what Milei says *about* institutional changes — justifications, announcements, framing of cuts or restructurings — not the changes themselves (those are documented separately). Code here when speech content directly references, justifies, or announces capability-eroding actions.

---

#### 2.1 Budgetary Retrenchment

References in discourse to budget cuts, spending reductions, or fiscal austerity affecting:

- **Budget_Cuts_Science**: Scientific institutions (CONICET, research programs, science budgets).

- **Budget_Cuts_Environment**: Environmental agencies, ministries, or sustainability programs.

- **Budget_Cuts_Sustainability_Programs**: Specific climate- or sustainability-related programs or initiatives.

- **Budget_Cuts_Universities**: Public universities or higher education institutions.

---

#### 2.2 Institutional Downgrading and Administrative Restructuring

References in discourse to organizational changes affecting sustainability or scientific governance:

- **Ministry_Reorganization**: Reorganization, downgrading (e.g., ministry → secretariat), or merging of environmental, energy, science, or sustainability ministries/secretariats.

- **Agency_Elimination_or_Merger**: Elimination or merger of specific agencies with sustainability or scientific mandates.

- **Regulatory_Institution_Changes**: Changes in regulatory bodies, oversight institutions, or enforcement agencies.

- **Administrative_Hollowing_Out**: Staffing reductions, early retirements, administrative restructurings that reduce institutional capacity without formal elimination.

---

#### 2.3 Sustainability-Policy Dismantling

References in discourse to changes in the policy instruments, commitments, or enforcement frameworks for sustainability:

- **Changes_in_Climate_Commitments**: References to changing, weakening, or abandoning formal climate targets, NDCs, or international commitments.

- **Withdrawal_from_International_Initiatives**: Withdrawal or distancing from multilateral sustainability initiatives, agreements, or governance bodies.

- **Policy_Instrument_Changes**: Changes in specific policy instruments (carbon pricing, emissions standards, renewable mandates, environmental impact assessments).

- **Deregulation_Environmental_Protection**: Deregulatory actions reducing environmental protections, permitting requirements, or conservation rules.

- **Reduced_Implementation_or_Enforcement**: References to non-enforcement, administrative delays, or reduced implementation of existing environmental laws or sustainability policies.

## Phase 2: Inductive (Emergent Categories)

The following categories were generated inductively during the v1 coding pass. They are carried forward as candidate codes for v2. Apply them when the pattern recurs, and evaluate whether they map cleanly onto v2 Dimension 1 sub-codes or remain distinct.

---

### Selective_Empiricism
**Definition:** A rhetorical pattern in which Milei deploys the language of empirical authority ("la evidencia empírica es incuestionable") exclusively for pro-market claims, while simultaneously characterizing opposing academic and institutional knowledge (including climate science) as ideologically captured and invalid. Distinct from Attack_on_Experts_and_Technocrats (which attacks the legitimacy of experts) because here the move is to *appropriate* empiricism for one side while denying it to the other. Recognize by the co-presence of strong empirical claims for libertarian positions alongside dismissal of expert institutions.

**V1 example:** *"La evidencia empírica es incuestionable"* (about capitalism's poverty-reduction record), followed by dismissal of "líderes, pensadores y académicos... amparados en un marco teórico equivocado" (Davos 2024).

**Closest v2 code:** 1.2 > Pseudo_Science_or_Ideological_Science — but apply as INDUCTIVE if the specific dual-move (appropriating empiricism + denying it) is the dominant feature.

---

### Temporal_Appropriation
**Definition:** A rhetorical strategy in which Milei co-opts the normative language of intergenerational responsibility — the moral foundation of sustainability discourse — and redeploys it to argue *against* state intervention and multilateral climate governance. The pattern involves appropriating future-oriented framing ("incendian el futuro para mantener caliente el presente") while directing that moral logic against the 2030 Agenda or environmental regulation. Distinct from Rejection_of_Multilateral_Governance (which attacks institutional legitimacy): Temporal_Appropriation captures the sustainability regime's own moral vocabulary and turns it against itself.

**V1 example:** *"incendian el futuro para mantener caliente el presente"* followed by rejection of the 2030 Agenda (UN 80th Assembly, 2025-09-24).

**Closest v2 code:** 1.1 > Rejection_of_Multilateral_Governance — but apply as INDUCTIVE if the temporal/moral-register appropriation is the dominant move.

---

### Reported_Denial
**Definition:** An opposing speaker's reported-speech paraphrase of a Milei climate statement, used as inter-discursive corroboration of a coded primary position. Apply when a political opponent accurately summarizes a Milei climate-related claim in a debate, providing evidence the position circulates publicly. Not primary discourse — function is confirmatory. Apply with INDUCTIVE prefix and note it is not primary Milei discourse.

**V1 example:** *"llegó a decir que el cambio climático es del socialismo"* — Bregman's paraphrase during the 2023 Presidential Debate.

## Instructions for the Analyst Agent

1. Read this codebook in full before touching any speech files.
2. Code using the v2 categories above. Do not import v1 codes directly — instead, map passages to the closest v2 code.
3. When a passage fits a carried-forward inductive category (Selective_Empiricism, Temporal_Appropriation, Reported_Denial), note both the INDUCTIVE label and the closest v2 Dimension/sub-code.
4. Save coded outputs to `data/processed/v2/coded/` (not v1 paths).
5. Track frequency in `data/processed/v2/frequency_tally_v2.json`.
6. If a new pattern emerges that isn't covered by any v2 code, follow the inductive category protocol: apply `[CODE: INDUCTIVE > TentativeName]`, document it, and add it to this codebook under Phase 2.
7. After coding the full corpus, generate `analysis/results/v2/final_analysis_report_v2.md`.
